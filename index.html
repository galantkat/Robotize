The Problem

Why companies can't
solve this alone.

The obligations are real and tightening. The tools to meet them remain fragmented, expensive, and unreliable.

Measurement Complexity
Scope 3 = 92% of emissions, yet unmeasured

The vast majority of corporate emissions sit in the value chain - supplier inputs, logistics, product use. Almost universally, these remain unquantified. GHG Protocol identifies supplier data as the single largest barrier to reliable disclosure, yet current tools require direct measurement that most suppliers will never provide.

Robotize: model-based Scope 3 approximation from your own operational inputs - no supplier surveys required.
Operational Fragmentation
Data scattered across every department

Emissions data lives in finance (energy bills), operations (production logs), procurement (supplier invoices), and supply chain systems - none of which talk to each other. Manual aggregation is slow, inconsistent year-on-year, and difficult to audit. The cost of in-house specialists and fragmented tools is rising as regulatory requirements intensify.

Robotize: one platform ingests operational data across all sources and generates a single, consistent emissions record.
IP-adjacent moat
Strategic partnerships

Beyond formal IP registration, Robotize's long-term defensibility will be meaningfully shaped by the network of relationships it builds with industry bodies, verifiers, and enterprise partners. Integrations with ERP systems, procurement platforms, and third-party audit firms embed Robotize into clients' operational workflows in ways that create high switching costs — a form of competitive protection that no patent can fully replicate. Partnerships with industry associations or sector-specific bodies (for example, Make UK or the British Chambers of Commerce) could also accelerate dataset growth by channelling anonymised emissions benchmarks across member cohorts, strengthening the data network effect identified in reinforcing loop R1. Crucially, these relationships carry implicit IP value: co-developed methodology, jointly validated benchmarks, or preferred-supplier agreements can be structured contractually to ensure Robotize retains ownership of any shared outputs. As Autio and Thomas (2026) note, the value of intangible assets is ultimately shaped by the business model built around them — and for a platform business like Robotize, the density and quality of its partner network may ultimately prove as defensible as the software itself.

Strategic Gap
Compliance ≠ Strategy. Measurement ≠ Action.

Most firms treat emissions reporting as a box-ticking obligation - a cost centre with no strategic upside. This misses the point. The companies that act early build investor confidence, unlock green finance, and win decarbonisation-linked RFPs. The signal you send to the market is shaped by the quality and credibility of your emissions data.

Robotize: turns compliance data into forward-looking reduction scenarios and actionable recommendations.

Business Model Dynamics

Three reinforcing loops.
Two managed constraints.

Robotize's growth model is self-reinforcing. Each new client improves the product, which attracts more clients - while onboarding friction and verification requirements are actively mitigated through product design.

Reinforcing · R1
Data Network Effect

The most strategically significant loop. Each new client expands the proprietary emissions dataset, improving AI model accuracy, which strengthens platform value and attracts further clients. This is the primary source of long-term defensibility.

New client→ Larger dataset→ Better AI accuracy→ Higher platform value→ More clients
Reinforcing · R2
Regulatory Tailwind

Tightening mandatory disclosure requirements under SECR, CSRD, and ISSB drive new sign-ups, deepen the dataset, improve compliance credibility, and strengthen enforcement that compels further adoption. The market creates its own momentum.

Regulation tightens→ Sign-ups increase→ Compliance credibility→ Regulatory trust
Reinforcing · R3
Trading Network Effect

As the accounting user base scales, verified credit data expands, improving buyer-seller matching and trading value in an integrated carbon marketplace. Activates once platform reaches critical mass - a future revenue diversification lever.

Larger user base→ Verified credit data→ Better matching→ Trading value
Balancing · B1
Onboarding Friction

Data input effort required of new clients temporarily dampens acquisition speed. Mitigated through self-serve UX design and product-led growth. This assumption is being actively tested - see the experiment page to participate.

Data input effort→ Slower acquisition→ Mitigated by UX
